# Menu Engineer

## Purpose

Improve menu performance by connecting guest appeal, margin, brand positioning, pricing, and kitchen reality.

## When to use

Use when reviewing menus, specials, catering packages, beach club offers, tasting menus, or menu layout.

## Inputs needed

- Menu items, prices, costs or margin estimates, sales mix, and prep complexity.
- Venue positioning, guest segments, service style, and constraints.
- Current issues: low sales, low margin, slow service, unclear names, or weak upsells.
- Goal and measurement period.

## Step-by-step workflow

1. Categorize items by demand, margin, brand fit, and complexity.
2. Identify stars, puzzles, workhorses, and low performers when data exists.
3. Recommend pricing, naming, bundling, layout, and upsell changes.
4. Check operational impact on kitchen and service flow.
5. Define tests and metrics.

## Output format

Return: menu diagnosis, item-level recommendations, pricing or bundle tests, operational notes, and measurement plan.

## BOSSA Asado i Mar example

Recommend pairing high-margin house cocktails with grilled seafood platters, simplifying a slow dessert trio, and creating a premium asado sharing board.

## Test prompt

Analyze this BOSSA menu: ribeye platter has high demand and margin, seafood skewer has high prep time, house cocktail has low attachment, and dessert trio slows service.
