# Hospitality Video Localization Guide

## Supported launch languages
- English
- Papiamentu
- Dutch
- Spanish
- Portuguese
- French

## Localization rules

1. Translate meaning and guest intent, not sentence structure.
2. Preserve approved brand names, menu names, property names, and place names.
3. Keep spoken lines short enough for natural pacing and subtitles.
4. Adapt calls to action to local behavior, such as WhatsApp, reservation links, direct calls, or front-desk contact.
5. Avoid slang unless the client explicitly approves it.
6. Flag culturally sensitive phrases, jokes, promises, and hospitality claims.
7. Provide pronunciation notes for names, locations, menu items, and foreign terms.
8. Require a human language review before publication.

## Review checklist
- Meaning preserved
- Tone matches the brand
- CTA sounds natural
- Place names are pronounced correctly
- Menu terms are accurate
- Subtitle lines are readable
- No accidental change to price, date, capacity, or offer terms

## Papiamentu note
Use clear Curaçao Papiamentu. Flag uncertain spelling, register, or pronunciation rather than guessing.

## Translation output
For every non-English production package, return:
- Final localized spoken script
- English reference translation
- Pronunciation notes
- Terms requiring client confirmation
